How a light sheet works

The basic orthogonal geometry — a sheet enters from the side, the camera looks from 90°.
The full LOCI path: laser → relay → galvo → knife-edge → cylindrical lens → excitation objective → sample, with detection at 90° to the pco.panda camera.

Core idea. Only the thin focal plane (~5 µm) is illuminated — not the whole sample. Fundamentally different from widefield and confocal.

The 90° detection. The camera sits at exactly 90° to the sheet, so it collects fluorescence with almost no laser background — clean, high-contrast images.

Why use it. Low phototoxicity, fast 3-D imaging, minimal photobleaching — ideal for large living or cleared samples like embryos and organoids.
