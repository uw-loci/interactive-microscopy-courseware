Light Sheet Alignment Simulation

A and B translate the light sheet (shift it without tilting); C and D pivot it about the waist (the thin point of the sheet stays put while the beam swings); E slides the waist along the beam. Push any too far and the beam walks off the objective's back aperture — then no light reaches the chamber. (Only these adjustments are shown; the real system has more.)

Heads-up: This is a simplified teaching simulation. Real alignment juggles many more variables — beam shape and power, multiple objectives, the light-sheet waist, angle and overlap with the detection focus, sample-induced aberrations, and more — and is considerably more complicated than the few controls shown here.
