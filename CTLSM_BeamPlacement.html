Place the beam in the chamber

The two steering mirrors aim the beam into the back of the excitation objective. Tilt them too far and the beam walks off the back aperture — then no light reaches the chamber. The E lens slides along the beam to set the waist, and also bends an off-centre beam part-way back toward the axis (but can't fully correct it).

A/B and C/D are the steering mirrors (the C/D fold mirror sits at −45°, aiming into the objective). E is the relay lens that slides to move the waist. Detection objective in yellow.
